Commercial Payloads and Starlink Deployment

Starship is designed to launch massive payloads that exceed the capacity of all other current launch vehicles. One of its primary commercial objectives is the deployment of SpaceX's next-generation Starlink V2 satellites, which are too large and heavy to be launched in large numbers by the Falcon 9.

To deploy these satellites, Starship features a specialized payload door mechanism, often called the 'pez dispenser'. The satellites are stacked vertically inside the payload bay and ejected one-by-one through a narrow slot. In addition to Starlink, Starship's massive 9-meter payload fairing allows it to launch large space telescopes, interplanetary science probes, and commercial lunar landers.
