Voyager 2's Jupiter Encounter

Voyager 2 made its closest approach to Jupiter on July 9, 1979, passing within 350,000 miles (570,000 kilometers) of the planet's cloud tops. Onboard cameras captured highly detailed images of Jupiter's atmosphere, revealing that the Great Red Spot is a complex, counterclockwise-rotating storm. The probe also mapped the atmospheric bands, capturing smaller eddies and storms.

During the flyby, Voyager 2 photographed Jupiter's moons Amalthea, Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto. The spacecraft conducted a 10-hour "volcano watch" on the moon Io, confirming Voyager 1's earlier discovery of active volcanism and showing how the volcanic features had changed in the four months between the two flybys. Together, the two probes observed nine erupting volcanoes on Io.
