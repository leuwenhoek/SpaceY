Voyager 2's Saturn Encounter

On August 26, 1981, Voyager 2 made its closest approach to Saturn. As it passed behind the planet, the spacecraft used its radio link to conduct an atmospheric occultation experiment, measuring how the radio signals were bent by Saturn's atmosphere to determine its temperature and pressure profiles.

Onboard instruments measured a temperature of -191 degrees Celsius (-312 degrees Fahrenheit) at a pressure of 70 millibars in the upper atmosphere, rising to -130 degrees Celsius (-202 degrees Fahrenheit) deeper in the atmosphere at 1,200 millibars. The spacecraft observed that Saturn's north pole was about 10 degrees Celsius cooler than mid-latitudes, which scientists attributed to seasonal variations. The probe also studied Saturn's rings and moon systems, capturing high-resolution photos of Enceladus, Tethys, and Dione.
