Future Trajectory and Stellar Passages

Voyager 2 is traveling on a trajectory that will carry it out of the solar system forever. The spacecraft is not heading toward any specific star system, but its path will bring it close to several stars over the next few hundred thousand years as it wanders the Milky Way.

In approximately 42,000 years, Voyager 2 will pass within 1.7 light-years of the red dwarf star Ross 248, which is located 10.3 light-years from Earth. If the spacecraft remains undisturbed, in about 296,000 years it will pass the bright star Sirius, the brightest star in Earth's night sky, at a distance of 4.3 light-years. The probe will continue to drift through space as a silent messenger from Earth.
