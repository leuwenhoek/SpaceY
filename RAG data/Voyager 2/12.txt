Voyager 2's Uranus Encounter

Voyager 2 made history on January 24, 1986, when it became the first human-made object to fly past Uranus, passing within 50,600 miles (81,500 kilometers) of the planet's cloud tops. During the encounter, the spacecraft discovered 11 previously unknown moons: Cordelia, Ophelia, Bianca, Cressida, Desdemona, Juliet, Portia, Rosalind, Belinda, Puck, and Perdita.

The spacecraft conducted detailed studies of Uranus's atmosphere, which is dominated by hydrogen and helium and features a unique tilt, with the planet's rotational axis inclined by 97.8 degrees. Voyager 2 also examined Uranus's thin ring system and discovered that the planet has a complex, highly tilted magnetic field that is misaligned with its rotational axis, along with a helix-shaped magnetic tail extending 10 million kilometers away from the Sun.
