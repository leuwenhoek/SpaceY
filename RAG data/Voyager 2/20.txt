The Deep Space Network and Interstellar Distances

As the Voyager probes travel deeper into interstellar space, they maintain contact with Earth through NASA's Deep Space Network (DSN). The DSN consists of three main antenna complexes located in Goldstone, California; Madrid, Spain; and Canberra, Australia, positioned approximately 120 degrees apart around the Earth to ensure continuous coverage.

Voyager 1 is currently located over 24 billion kilometers (15 billion miles) from Earth, while Voyager 2 is over 20 billion kilometers (12 billion miles) away. At these vast distances, radio signals traveling at the speed of light take hours to reach the spacecraft, making real-time communications impossible and requiring high levels of autonomy from the onboard systems.
