Voyager 2's Neptune Encounter

On August 25, 1989, Voyager 2 conducted its final planetary encounter, flying past Neptune. Mission controllers routed the spacecraft close to Neptune's north pole, passing just 3,080 miles (4,950 kilometers) above the planet's cloud tops. This close trajectory allowed the spacecraft to use Neptune's gravity to bend its path southward toward Triton, Neptune's largest moon.

Voyager 2 passed within 25,000 miles (40,000 kilometers) of Triton, capturing detailed images of its icy surface, which features a "cantaloupe terrain" and active nitrogen geysers. At Neptune, the spacecraft discovered five new moons, four rings, and a massive storm system called the Great Dark Spot, which featured winds of up to 1,500 miles per hour, the fastest winds recorded in the solar system.
