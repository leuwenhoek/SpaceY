The Interstellar Mission and Nova Cygni

After completing its planetary flybys in 1989, Voyager 2 began its interstellar mission, designed to study the environment at the outer edge of the solar system and beyond. Today, the spacecraft continues to transmit scientific data from interstellar space.

In 1992, Voyager 2 utilized its Ultraviolet Spectrometer (UVS) to observe the nova V1974 Cygni. This observation was the first time a nova had been observed in the far-ultraviolet spectrum, providing astrophysicists with unique data on the temperature and energy output of the stellar explosion. This demonstrated how the spacecraft's instruments could be repurposed for astronomical observations.
