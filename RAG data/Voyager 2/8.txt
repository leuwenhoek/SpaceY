Attitude Control and Propulsion Systems

Due to the energy required to launch the 825-kilogram (1,819-pound) Voyager 2 spacecraft toward Jupiter, it was equipped with a temporary propulsion module. This module included a solid-rocket motor and eight hydrazine engines to provide attitude control during ascent and launch burns, and was jettisoned shortly after the successful launch phase.

For attitude control during its long journey, Voyager 2 uses sixteen Aerojet MR-103 thrusters. Four thrusters are used for trajectory correction maneuvers, while the other twelve are split into two redundant branches of six thrusters to stabilize the spacecraft on its three axes. The thrusters are supplied by a single 70-centimeter spherical titanium tank containing 100 kilograms of hydrazine at launch, which is estimated to last until 2034.
