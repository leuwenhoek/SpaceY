Voyager 2 Spacecraft Design and Systems

Constructed by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, Voyager 2 features a central decagonal prism bus that houses its electronics, propulsion tanks, and computers. To maintain its orientation in space, the spacecraft uses a three-axis stabilization system. This system is part of the Attitude and Articulation Control Subsystem (AACS), which processes inputs from gyroscopes, a Sun sensor, and a Canopus star tracker to monitor the spacecraft's attitude.

The AACS controls pointing by firing small hydrazine thrusters, keeping the high-gain antenna oriented toward Earth. Voyager 2 was equipped with 11 scientific instruments, including imaging cameras, magnetometers, cosmic-ray detectors, and spectrometers. Most of these instruments are mounted on booms to prevent interference from the spacecraft's electronic systems, with the cameras mounted on a steerable scan platform.
