Comparing the Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 Missions

The Voyager program consists of two identical spacecraft, Voyager 1 and Voyager 2, which were launched in 1977. While Voyager 2 was launched first, Voyager 1 was placed on a faster, more direct trajectory that allowed it to reach Jupiter and Saturn earlier.

Voyager 2's trajectory was designed to visit all four giant outer planets, which required a longer flight path. Voyager 1's trajectory was optimized for a close flyby of Saturn's moon Titan, which deflected the spacecraft out of the ecliptic plane, preventing it from visiting Uranus or Neptune. As a result, Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to have visited all four outer planets, while Voyager 1 remains the most distant human-made object.
