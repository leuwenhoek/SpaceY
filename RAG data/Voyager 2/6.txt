Communications and Data Recording

To transmit data over billions of miles, Voyager 2 is equipped with a 3.7-meter (12-foot) parabolic high-gain antenna. The spacecraft communicates with NASA's Deep Space Network using S-band radio frequencies for uplink commands and X-band frequencies for downlink telemetry, with an S-band transmitter available as a backup. At the distance of Jupiter, the communications system could transmit data at rates up to 115.2 kilobits per second.

As Voyager 2 travels farther from Earth, its data rate decreases due to the inverse-square law, dropping to about 160 bits per second in interstellar space. When the spacecraft is out of view of Earth's antennas or cannot establish a real-time link, it uses an onboard digital tape recorder (DTR). The DTR can store up to 64 megabytes of scientific data, which is later played back and transmitted when a communication link is established.
