Power Management and Instrument Shutdowns

Voyager 2 is powered by three Multi-Hundred Watt Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators (MHW-RTGs) that use Plutonium-238. At launch, the RTGs provided 470 watts of electrical power. Because Plutonium-238 decays and the thermocouples degrade, the power output decreases by about 4 watts per year. By the 2020s, the power level had dropped to a point where controllers had to begin deactivating systems.

In April 2023, JPL engineers implemented a plan to draw power from a small backup reservoir designed for a voltage regulator safety mechanism. This allowed all five of the spacecraft's active science instruments to continue operating through 2026. However, in October 2024, NASA deactivated the Plasma Science (PLS) instrument, which had become less effective in interstellar space, to conserve power. This measure will help keep the remaining four science instruments functioning into the 2030s.
