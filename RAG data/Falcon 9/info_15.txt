The Historic Demo-2 Crewed Launch

On May 30, 2020, SpaceX and NASA made history with the launch of the Demo-2 mission. A Falcon 9 rocket lifted off from Launch Complex 39A at NASA's Kennedy Space Center in Florida, carrying NASA astronauts Bob Behnken and Doug Hurley inside a Crew Dragon capsule.

This flight marked the first time that astronauts were launched into orbit on a commercial rocket, and the first crewed launch from U.S. soil since the final Space Shuttle flight in 2011. The Falcon 9 performed flawlessly, delivering the crew into orbit before its first-stage booster landed successfully on the drone ship Of Course I Still Love You. The mission was a complete success, paving the way for regular operational crew rotations to the ISS.
