Payload Fairing Recovery and Reuse

The payload fairing is the protective clamshell structure at the top of the Falcon 9 that shields satellites from aerodynamic forces and heating during launch. Once the rocket leaves the atmosphere, the fairing halves separate and fall back to Earth.

To reduce costs, SpaceX recovers and reuses these fairings. Each half is equipped with cold-gas thrusters for attitude control and a steerable parafoil that deploys to slow its descent. Specialized recovery ships recover the fairing halves from the ocean surface after they splash down. By successfully reusing fairings, which cost approximately $6 million per flight to manufacture, SpaceX has further reduced the cost of commercial space launches.
