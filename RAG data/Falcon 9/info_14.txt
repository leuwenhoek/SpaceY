The NASA Commercial Crew Program

Building on the success of the cargo program, NASA established the Commercial Crew Program to restore America's capability to launch astronauts into space. In 2014, NASA selected SpaceX to develop a human-rated crew transportation system based on the Falcon 9 rocket and the Crew Dragon spacecraft.

Human-rating the Falcon 9 required extensive modifications to the rocket, including upgrading the pressure vessels on the second stage and implementing a highly advanced abort system. The system features SuperDraco engines built into the Crew Dragon capsule, which can pull the astronauts safely away from the Falcon 9 rocket in the event of an emergency during launch or ascent. This system successfully met NASA's rigorous safety standards.
