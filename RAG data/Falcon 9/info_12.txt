Evolution of Falcon 9 Versions

Since its debut in 2010, the Falcon 9 has undergone significant design changes to increase its payload capacity, reliability, and reusability. The initial version, Falcon 9 v1.0, featured a 3x3 engine grid and lower thrust Merlin 1C engines. In 2013, SpaceX introduced Falcon 9 v1.1, which featured a stretched tank structure, the Octaweb engine layout, and the more powerful Merlin 1D engines.

Subsequent upgrades led to the Falcon 9 Full Thrust (v1.2) in 2015, which introduced subcooled propellants to pack more fuel into the tanks, and the Block 4 version in 2017. The final evolution, Falcon 9 Block 5, was introduced in 2018. Designed specifically to meet NASA's safety requirements for human spaceflight, Block 5 features upgraded heat shields, titanium grid fins, and structural reinforcements that allow boosters to fly up to ten times or more with minimal maintenance.
