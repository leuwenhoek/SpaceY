The Far Future of Voyager 1

Voyager 1 is traveling on a hyperbolic trajectory that will carry it out of the solar system forever. Provided it does not collide with interstellar debris, the spacecraft is expected to reach the inner edge of the Oort Cloud—a vast, spherical shell of icy comets surrounding the solar system—in about 300 years. It will take the probe approximately 30,000 years to pass completely through the Oort Cloud.

Although Voyager 1 is not heading toward any specific star system, in about 40,000 years it will pass within 1.6 light-years (approximately 100,000 astronomical units) of the red dwarf star Gliese 445. This star, located in the constellation Camelopardalis, is currently moving toward our solar system. Long after its transmitters fall silent due to lack of electrical power, Voyager 1 will continue to wander the Milky Way as a silent monument to human curiosity.
