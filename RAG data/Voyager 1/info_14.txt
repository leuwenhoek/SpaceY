The Voyager Golden Record: Visuals and Selection Committee

In addition to audio recordings, the Voyager Golden Record contains 115 images designed to provide a visual portrait of Earth and human civilization. These images include mathematical definitions, chemical formulas showing the building blocks of life, diagrams of human anatomy, photographs of landscapes, cities, animals, and people engaged in various activities. The images are encoded in analog format, and instructions on the record cover detail how to reconstruct them.

The selection of the record's contents was managed by a committee chaired by astronomer Carl Sagan of Cornell University. The committee, which included science writer Timothy Ferris and artist Jon Lomberg, spent months gathering materials that would represent the best of humanity. The record also includes printed messages from U.S. President Jimmy Carter and UN Secretary-General Kurt Waldheim, expressing a message of peace and cooperation to any intelligence that might find the spacecraft in the future.
