The Voyager Golden Record: Design and Audio Contents

To share a message from Earth with any potential extraterrestrial civilizations, both Voyager spacecraft carry a 12-inch (30-centimeter) gold-plated copper phonograph record. The record is encased in a protective aluminum jacket, on which instructions are engraved using symbolic diagrams. These diagrams explain how to play the record and decode the images, and include a pulsar map that pinpoints the location of Earth relative to 14 pulsars, using the transition frequency of hydrogen as a cosmic clock.

The audio content of the Golden Record was designed to represent the diversity of life and culture on Earth. It opens with spoken greetings in 55 different languages, ranging from ancient Sumerian to modern English. Following the greetings, the record features a 35-minute compilation of natural sounds, including wind, rain, thunder, bird songs, whale vocalizations, heartbeats, and human laughter. The musical section contains 90 minutes of diverse compositions, featuring classical works by Bach, Mozart, and Beethoven, alongside traditional folk music from around the world, and contemporary pieces such as Chuck Berry's "Johnny B. Goode" and Blind Willie Johnson's "Dark Was the Night, Cold Was the Ground."
