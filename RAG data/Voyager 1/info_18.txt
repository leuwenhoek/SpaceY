Spacecraft Power Management and Instrument Shutdowns

As Voyager 1 travels deeper into interstellar space, managing its dwindling power supply has become the primary challenge for mission controllers. Because the electrical power generated by the spacecraft's RTGs decreases by approximately 4 watts each year, engineers have had to implement strict power-saving measures. This includes systematically turning off heaters that keep the scientific instruments warm and deactivating the instruments themselves.

In December 2017, NASA engineers successfully fired Voyager 1's four Trajectory Correction Maneuver (TCM) thrusters for the first time since 1980. These thrusters were used in place of the primary attitude control thrusters, which had degraded, to keep the spacecraft's antenna pointed toward Earth, extending the mission's life. In April 2026, as part of ongoing power conservation efforts, NASA shut down the Low-Energy Charged Particles (LECP) instrument, leaving only three active science instruments operating on the spacecraft.
