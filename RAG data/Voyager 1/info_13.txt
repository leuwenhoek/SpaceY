Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators (RTGs) and Power Decay

Because the Voyager probes travel too far from the Sun to utilize solar panels, they rely on Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators (RTGs) for electrical power. Each spacecraft is equipped with three Multi-Hundred Watt RTGs (MHW-RTGs) that use the heat generated by the natural radioactive decay of Plutonium-238. At launch, the RTGs provided approximately 470 watts of electrical power at 30 volts DC.

Plutonium-238 decays with a half-life of 87.74 years, which causes the thermal heat output of the RTGs to decrease by about 0.79% each year. Additionally, the silicon-germanium thermocouples that convert this heat into electrical energy degrade over time. By 2011, the power output of the RTGs had declined to about 76% of their launch capacity. This continuous decline in electrical power requires mission controllers to carefully manage the spacecraft's power budget, systematically turning off heaters and non-essential instruments to prolong the mission's life.
