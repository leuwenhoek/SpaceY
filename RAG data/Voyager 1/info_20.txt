Milestones and the Light-Day Mark

Since surpassing the previous record-holder, NASA's Pioneer 10 explorer, in February 1998, Voyager 1 has been the farthest human-made object in existence. The spacecraft continues to operate more than 48 years after its launch, sending back scientific data from interstellar space. This longevity is a testament to the robust design and engineering of the probe.

Voyager 1 is approaching another significant milestone. Around November 13, 2026, the spacecraft will reach a distance of one "light-day" from Earth. A light-day is the distance that light travels in a vacuum in 24 hours, equivalent to approximately 16.2 billion miles (26.1 billion kilometers). When this milestone is reached, a radio signal sent from Earth will take a full 24 hours to reach Voyager 1, and the spacecraft's reply will take another 24 hours, resulting in a 48-hour round-trip communication delay.
